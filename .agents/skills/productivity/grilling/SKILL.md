# Grilling
Interview the user relentlessly about every aspect of the idea until those four items are settled. Walk down each branch of the decision tree, resolving dependencies one-by-one. For each question, provide your recommended answer.

Ask the questions one at a time, waiting for feedback on each question before continuing. Asking multiple questions at once is bewildering.

If a fact can be found by exploring the environment (filesystem, tools, etc.), look it up rather than asking the user. The decisions, though, are the user's — put each one to them and wait for an answer.

The agent owns readiness: keep asking until it can articulate outcome, non-goals, constraints, and verifiable success criteria without inventing them. Proceed only when remaining uncertainty cannot materially change the approach.

Do not hand off to `$requirements` while material intent, terminology, success criteria, or constraints remain unanswered.
